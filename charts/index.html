Primary Failure Cause Distribution

Demand-side failures dominate. PMF, no-urgent-pain, and business-model-mismatch together account for 34% of cases.

Source Quality

72% of cases are based on primary sources — founder postmortems, interviews, or official announcements.

Confidence Distribution

69% of cases are high-confidence. Medium cases are transparently flagged in the dataset.

Stage at Failure

81% of cases are bootstrapped or seed. The dataset is genuinely early-stage.

Region Distribution

North America is overrepresented at 71%. This is a dataset limitation, not a true global distribution.

Category Distribution

SaaS, marketplaces, and consumer apps represent 59% of cases. Hardware, AI, and edtech need more coverage.

Category vs Primary Failure Cause

SaaS failures cluster around demand problems. Marketplace failures cluster around operations and liquidity. Social failures cluster around trust and retention.

Stage vs Primary Failure Cause

Bootstrapped startups fail on willingness-to-pay. Funded Series A startups fail on operational complexity and unit economics.

Source Quality vs Confidence

Primary sources almost always yield high-confidence classifications (68 of 72). Secondary sources are mostly medium-confidence (27 of 28).

Failure Clusters

Demand failure is the largest cluster (34%). Execution failure is the second (20%). Legal/trust/platform combined account for 14%.
