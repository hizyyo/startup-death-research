## Batch Design

Batch 004 intentionally rebalances the dataset after Batch 003, which included more famous VC-backed and secondary-source cases.

Primary goals:

- increase smaller bootstrapped and seed-stage representation
- increase founder-interview and founder-postmortem evidence
- add more non-US examples
- add more founder-conflict, market-too-small, no-urgent-pain, and marketplace-liquidity cases
- reduce dependence on famous startup failures

## Strongest Cases In This Batch

- Delite: detailed founder interview with clear nice-to-have / workflow-change failure.
- Eventloot: founder interview with concrete customer-discovery and usage evidence.
- MyCity: strong govtech non-existing-market case.
- DynaDomains: concrete acquisition attempts and zero-demand marketplace evidence.
- MotionThink: strong co-founder misalignment case.
- Ansaro: strong enterprise SaaS pilot-contract / PMF failure case.
- RingDaddy: excellent fast no-code validation and trust/behavior mismatch case.

## Source Quality

Most Batch 004 cases use one of two source patterns:

- Failory founder interviews.
- Indie Hackers founder shutdown posts.

These are treated as primary or near-primary because the failure explanation comes directly from the founder, even when hosted/interviewed by a third-party platform.

## Quality Notes

- Some Indie Hackers cases are short, but they are valuable because they represent very small failures usually missing from public datasets.
- Some dates and funding fields remain `unknown` where the source did not specify enough detail.
- This batch improves the dataset's admissions/research quality by showing that the project is not just a list of famous failures.
