# Limitations

# The Anatomy of Startup Death

## Purpose

This document records limitations honestly so the project reads as serious research rather than overclaiming.

## Core Limitations

## Public-Source Bias

The dataset only includes failures with public evidence.

This creates bias toward founders who wrote postmortems, appeared in interviews, or received media coverage.

Silent failures are underrepresented.

## Founder-Narrative Bias

Many sources are written by founders after failure.

Founder accounts are valuable but not neutral. They may understate interpersonal conflict, investor pressure, poor execution, or market misunderstanding.

## Regional Bias

North America dominates the dataset.

This reflects the availability of English-language postmortems and startup media coverage, not necessarily the true global distribution of startup failure causes.

## Category Bias

SaaS, marketplaces, consumer apps, and social products are overrepresented.

Hardware, biotech, deeptech, infrastructure, and non-internet businesses are underrepresented.

## Survivorship Of Documentation

The dataset studies failures that left traces.

It does not capture the many startups that shut down without a public explanation.

## Classification Uncertainty

Failure causes are often multi-causal.

The `primary_failure_cause` field is a structured interpretation, not an objective fact.

The `secondary_failure_causes` field is used to preserve complexity.

## Stage Ambiguity

Some startups are difficult to classify by stage because sources do not always disclose funding, team size, or fundraising history.

## Not Predictive

The dataset should not be presented as a startup death prediction model.

It is a descriptive research dataset for studying patterns in public failure narratives.

## Public Framing

Recommended wording:

> This dataset does not claim to represent all startup failures. It maps patterns in public startup postmortems and shutdown narratives using a structured taxonomy.

Avoid wording:

> This dataset explains why startups fail.

Better wording:

> This dataset studies how startup failures are described, evidenced, and classified in public postmortem sources.
