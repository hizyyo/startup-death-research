# Positioning

# The Anatomy of Startup Death

## A Dataset and Taxonomy of Early-Stage Startup Failures

## Project Type

Open public research project.

This is not a newsletter, blog, startup database, or content curation project. It is a structured research infrastructure built around data, taxonomy, methodology, evidence, and public access.

## Core Idea

Most startup education focuses on success stories, which creates survivor bias. Founders study companies that survived, but often learn less from the companies that failed. This project studies failed early-stage startups through public postmortems, founder essays, interviews, news archives, startup databases, and other public sources.

The goal is to identify recurring patterns of startup failure and make those patterns visible, searchable, comparable, and useful.

## Core Research Question

**EN:**

What recurring patterns explain early-stage startup failure, and can these patterns be systematically classified across market type, business model, pricing, go-to-market strategy, retention, and founder decisions?

**RU:**

Какие повторяющиеся паттерны объясняют провал early-stage стартапов, и можно ли системно классифицировать эти паттерны по типу рынка, бизнес-модели, pricing, go-to-market strategy, retention и founder decisions?

## Secondary Research Questions

- Which failure causes appear most often in early-stage startup postmortems?
- Do different business models fail for different reasons?
- Are B2B, B2C, SaaS, marketplace, consumer app, and devtool startups associated with different failure patterns?
- What early warning symptoms appear before shutdown?
- Can failed startups be grouped into recognizable failure archetypes?
- Can a similarity-based tool help current founders identify historical failure patterns similar to their own startup?

## What This Project Is

- A public research project.
- A structured dataset.
- A taxonomy of startup failure causes.
- A methodology for classifying startup deaths.
- A public website for exploring the data.
- A research report.
- An experimental similarity tool.

## What This Project Is Not

- Not a motivational startup blog.
- Not a listicle of failed companies.
- Not an AI tool that predicts failure.
- Not a paid database first.
- Not a collection of random stories without sources.
- Not a claim that startup failure has one simple cause.

## Target Audience

- Early-stage founders.
- Indie hackers.
- Student entrepreneurs.
- Startup accelerators.
- Entrepreneurship researchers.
- University admissions reviewers.
- Investors interested in founder education.

## Core Value Proposition

Founders usually learn from success stories. This project helps them learn from structured failure patterns.

## Final Positioning Statement

The Anatomy of Startup Death is a public research infrastructure for studying how early-stage startups fail. It transforms scattered startup postmortems into a structured dataset, taxonomy, and analysis of recurring failure patterns.
