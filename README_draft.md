# The Anatomy of Startup Death

A structured public dataset and taxonomy of early-stage startup failures.

## Overview

The Anatomy of Startup Death is a research project that collects, classifies, and analyzes public startup failure postmortems.

The MVP dataset contains 100 startup failure cases, each coded by failure cause, source quality, stage, business model, category, geography, and evidence strength.

## Why This Exists

Most startup failure discussions are anecdotal.

This project turns public postmortems into structured research data.

The goal is not to mock failed founders or predict startup death. The goal is to understand recurring failure patterns with evidence and transparency.

`data/startup_failures_mvp.csv`

Current version:

- 0 validation issues

## Taxonomy

Each case is assigned one primary failure cause and optional secondary causes.

Example categories include:

- product-market fit failure
- no urgent pain
- distribution failure
- business model mismatch
- marketplace liquidity failure
- operational complexity
- technical complexity
- platform risk
- regulatory or legal pressure
- trust and safety failure

## Methodology

Cases are included when they have enough public evidence to support classification.

Preferred sources:

- founder postmortems
- founder interviews
- official shutdown announcements
- detailed reporting with founder quotes
- legal or regulatory sources for legal cases

Each row includes a confidence score and an evidence summary.

## Limitations

This dataset is not comprehensive.

It is biased toward startups with public postmortems, English-language coverage, and visible online traces.

North American startups are overrepresented.

Failure causes are often multi-causal, so classifications should be read as structured interpretations rather than absolute truth.
